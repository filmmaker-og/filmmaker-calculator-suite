## PHASE 1: THE FREE CALCULATOR

### Price: $0 — Always free. Forever.

This is not a product. This is a **funnel.** It's the thing that gets found on Google, shared in Facebook groups, linked in film school Discord servers, and bookmarked by every aspiring producer.

### What It Does

The calculator walks the user through 4 chapters:

| Chapter | What They Input | What They Learn |
|---|---|---|
| **01 — Budget** | Production budget + guild signatories (SAG, DGA, WGA) | What their film actually costs to make |
| **02 — Capital Stack** | Where the money comes from: tax credits, senior debt, gap/mezzanine debt, equity investment, deferments | How a film is financed from multiple sources |
| **03 — The Deal** | What a distributor might pay for the finished film | Whether the deal breaks even or not |
| **04 — The Waterfall** | Nothing — it calculates automatically | Who gets paid, how much, in what order |

### The Calculation Engine

The waterfall follows real film financing convention:

1. **Off-the-Top** (paid first from gross revenue):
   - CAM: 1% of gross
   - Sales/distribution fee: User-defined %
   - Guild reserves: SAG 4.5%, WGA 1.2%, DGA 1.2% (if selected)
   - Marketing & delivery cap: Fixed amount

2. **Debt Service** (paid from remaining):
   - Senior debt: Principal × (1 + interest rate)
   - Gap/Mezzanine debt: Principal × (1 + interest rate)

3. **Equity Recoupment** (paid from remaining):
   - Equity: Principal × (1 + preferred return %)

4. **Deferments** (paid from remaining):
   - Fixed deferred compensation amount

5. **Profit Pool** (50/50 split):
   - Producer: 50% of remaining
   - Investor: 50% of remaining + 100% of prior equity recoupment

### The Critical Moment

By the end of Chapter 4, the user is staring at a fully calculated waterfall distribution — the exact financial model that entertainment lawyers charge thousands to build.

They see their investor's return. They see their own profit. They see the whole picture.

And then they think: **"I need to show this to someone."**

They can't. It's on a screen. It's not a document. It's not shareable. It's not presentable.

**That's where the money starts.**
