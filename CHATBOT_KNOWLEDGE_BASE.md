The calculator is **completely free**. No account required. No credit card. No paywall on the simulator itself. The paid products are professional export packages.

## 6. KEY FILM FINANCE CONCEPTS

### Negative Cost
The all-in cost to produce the film. This is the number you need to raise.

### Capital Stack
The combination of funding sources: tax credits, senior debt, gap loans, equity. Each sits in a specific repayment position.

### Waterfall
The strict priority order in which revenue flows to stakeholders. Like buckets — money fills the top bucket first, only overflows to the next when full. The producer is at the bottom.

**Waterfall order:**
1. CAM (1% off top)
2. Sales Agent Fee + Marketing Expenses
3. Guild Residuals (SAG 4.5%, DGA 1.2%, WGA 1.2%)
4. Senior Debt (principal + interest, typically 10%)
5. Gap/Mezzanine Debt (principal + interest, typically 18%)
6. Equity Investors (principal + preferred return, typically 20%)
7. Deferments
8. Profit Pool — split 50/50 (investor pool / producer pool)

### Senior Debt
Bank loans, lowest risk, paid back first. Typical rate: 5-10%.

### Gap/Mezzanine
Bridge financing, higher risk than bank. Typical rate: 15-20%.

### Equity
Private investment. Highest risk. Gets principal back + premium (usually 20%) before 50/50 profit split.

### Off-the-Top
CAM, sales fees, marketing, guild residuals. These come out before anyone in the waterfall gets paid. On a $2M acquisition, this can easily be 20-30% ($400K-$600K).

### Breakeven
The exact revenue number needed to pay back all debts, equity, and fees.

### Multiple
Investor ROI metric. Total return divided by original investment. 2.0x+ is great, below 1.0x means they lost money.

## 7. FULL GLOSSARY (100+ Terms)

The chatbot should be able to define any of these. Key terms users are most likely to ask about:

- **Above the Line (ATL):** Writer, Director, Producer, Principal Cast costs. Usually 20-35% of budget.
- **Below the Line (BTL):** Crew, Equipment, Locations. Usually 45-60% of budget.
- **CAM (Collection Account Manager):** Neutral third party distributing revenue. ~1% fee. Protects investors.
- **Chain of Title:** Legal docs proving you own the rights. Can't sell a film without it.
- **Completion Bond:** Insurance guaranteeing the film gets finished. Banks require it.
- **Cross-Collateralization:** TRAP — distributor uses your film's profits to cover another film's losses. Always demand "Single Picture Accounting."
- **Deliverables:** Technical masters, audio, captions, artwork distributors require. Budget $30K–$75K.
- **E&O Insurance (Errors & Omissions):** Required by distributors before release. Covers libel/copyright claims.
- **Minimum Guarantee (MG):** Cash advance from distributor against future revenue.
- **Pre-Sales:** Selling distribution rights before the film is made to raise production money.
- **Residuals:** Royalties paid to guild members for reruns/secondary markets.
- **Sales Agent:** Broker who sells distribution rights internationally. Fee: 10-25%.
- **Tax Credit:** Government rebate on qualifying production spend. Typical: 20-35% of budget.
- **Pari Passu:** "On Equal Footing." Investors in the same tier get paid back simultaneously.
- **Pay or Play:** Guarantee that talent gets paid even if the film is cancelled.
- **Single Picture Accounting:** Each film accounted for separately. Prevents cross-collateralization.
- **Soft Money:** Tax credits, grants, subsidies — non-recoupable funding.
- **Force Majeure:** Contract clause allowing cancellation due to unforeseen events (war, pandemic).

## 8. HOW THE CALCULATOR WORKS (For user hand-holding)

### Step 1 — Budget
Enter your production budget (negative cost). Toggle union signatory status (SAG, DGA, WGA). Quick-select amounts available: $250K, $750K, $1.5M, $2.5M, $5M.

### Step 2 — Capital Stack
Select your funding sources. For each, enter amount and interest/fee rate. The app shows your funding gap or surplus in real time. Most indie films use Senior Debt + Equity.

Available sources:
- **Tax Credits** (reduces cost, typical 20-35% of budget)
- **Senior Debt** (bank/presale loan, first position, typical 10% interest)
- **Gap/Mezzanine** (higher risk subordinate debt, typical 18% interest)
- **Equity Investment** (private investors, last in, typical 20% preferred return)
- **Deferred Compensation** (producer fees, talent defers, paid after equity)

### Step 3 — Deal Structure
Enter expected acquisition price (what a buyer would pay for the film). Set sales agent fee (10-20% typical), marketing expenses ($75K standard cap). See live calculation of net-to-waterfall.

**Pro tip:** Start with 2x your budget as the acquisition price baseline.

### Step 4 — Waterfall
Everything calculates automatically. See who gets paid, in what order, and whether your investors make money. Color-coded verdict system:
- < 0.5x Multiple = "Terrible"
- 0.5x–0.99x = "Poor" / "Below Average"
- 1.0x = "Breakeven"
- 1.0x–1.5x = "Good"
- 1.5x–2.0x = "Very Good"
- 2.0x+ = "Great"

### Demo Mode
Users can run a demo simulation ($1M budget, $2.5M revenue) without entering any data to understand the concept first.

## 9. BUDGET BENCHMARKS

| Tier | Range | Characteristics | Typical Acquisition |
|---|---|---|---|
| Micro/Low | $100K–$1M | Lean crews, deferred pay, limited locations | $250K–$800K |
| Mid-Budget | $1M–$5M | Union or hybrid, modest talent | $1M–$3M |
| Elevated Indie | $5M–$15M | Name cast required, full union | Territory-by-territory deals |

**Rule of Thumb:** Realistic acquisition = 0.8x–1.5x budget for first-timers. 2x–3x with a track record.

**Budget Composition:**
- Above-the-Line: 20-35%
- Below-the-Line: 45-60%
- Post-Production: 10-15%
- Contingency + Insurance: 10-12%

**Common Mistakes:**
- Underestimating post-production (budget 15%, not 5%)
- No contingency buffer (10% is survival, not padding)
- Ignoring delivery requirements ($30K–$75K for deliverables)
